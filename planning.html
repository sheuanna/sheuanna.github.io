Planning the hike

1. The Lottery

Hiking up to the top Half Dome requires a permit for the final ascent on the cables, and only a limited number of permits are available per day via a lottery system. We wanted to be able to hike Half Dome on Memorial Day weekend (the most popular weekend to visit Yosmite). Given the uncertainty of the lottery, we came up with a system to "game" the lottery. The lottery rules incidated that each member of our group could only enter the lottery once as "trip leader" and on each entry we needed to designate a "deputy". When using the permit the "trip leader" or "deputy" must be present and the permit is not transferrable. However, the rules did not state there was a limit to how many times a person could be listed as "deputy", so to game the lottery we had our friends and family enter the lottery as "trip leader", while putting one of our names as "deputy" allowing us to enter the lottery many times more than the rules stated.

Based on historical numbers we calculated our original odds and our new odds of winning a permit by "gaming" the lottery date:

Date of permit Original odds New odds
Saturday, May 28 3% 45%
Sunday May 29 3% 55%
Monday, May 30 7% 100%

2. The Supplies

We needed to bring enough supplies for the 13 hour hike, including enough food an water as none would be available on the trail. On our way to Yosemite we made a stop at Walmart for the following supplies:

  • Water
  • Cliff bars
  • Beef jerky
  • Bananas
  • Bagels
  • Suncreen
  • Bug repellent
  • Grippy, rubbery, football gloves for climbing the cables to protect our hands
